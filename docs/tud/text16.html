Dr. Olga Kyselova (f)

Professor for Digital City Science at the CityScienceLab at Hafencity University Hamburg and director of the WISSENSARCHITEKTUR Laboratory of Knowledge Architecture at the TU Dresden. Between 1998 and 2001, he practiced as a freelance and employed architect in Tokyo, among others at Ishiyama Experimental Underground Architecture Factory and Arata Isozaki & Associates. From 2001, he was a Research Associate at TU Dresden, where he was later appointed Junior Professor for Knowledge Architecture (2009-2015). He has taught at different universities in Japan, USA, China, Italy, Switzerland, and Angola. He was Visiting Professor at Universita degli Studi dell'Aquila and at ISEN Toulon. He has published several books and more than 100 scientific papers and essays. He has won several prizes, scholarships and awards, incl. the Grand Prize of the European Association for Architecture Education (EAAE).

He is a DAAD scholar, currently on exchange studies at the WISSENSARCHITEKTUR - Knowledge Architecture Lab, on DAAD's collaboration with IITR. He is an undergraduate student at IIT Roorkee. He is a mentor at the Artificial Intelligence and Electronics Section, and the Product design Section of the Students’ Technical Council, IIT Roorkee. As a member of these student groups, he was involved in the internal zoning of the Students‘ Activity Center, IIT Roorkee. He was the project leader for Campus Sustainability research at IIT Roorkee, and worked along with Co-Plug UK, on the DesTech Smart city strategy project which is implemented in their Social Infrastructure Demand Management (SIDM) Systems. He was involved in organising one of Asia’s largest hackathons -Smart India Hackathon, and convened the event through one of its nodal centers, in IIT Roorkee. He has also worked on diverse projects on architecture, soft robotics and kirigami.
